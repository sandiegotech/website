Three ways to take part.

SDIT exists to reunite knowledge across disciplines in pursuit of truth, human flourishing, and the long-term success of civilization. You can take part at three depths — and the first is open to everyone, today.

Learn from the work as a Visitor. Join the in-person circle as a Student. Help build the institution as a Fellow.

StudentThe in-person circle

Come to learn, in person.

Students are the small in-person circle — people who come to SDIT itself to learn: in the workshop, the seminar room, and the water. It is not a degree, and not yet a credential. It is a real education in good company, during the founding.

Places are few, and held to a high bar — not of résumé, but of seriousness. A Student is expected to show up, do the work, and raise the level of everyone around them. Cost is never the deciding factor; if support is needed, say so plainly.

FellowBuild the institution

The deepest level of involvement.

A Founding Fellowship is not a degree, a bootcamp, or a credential. It is an invitation to serious work — on real questions and real projects — in the company of people figuring out what it means to think and build well in the age of AI.

Fellows are not students passing through a finished curriculum. They are founders of one. What a Fellow builds becomes part of how the institution is built. There is no tuition — Fellowships stay open regardless of financial circumstance. The only requirement is genuine commitment to the work.

SupportMake it possible

Fund the founding.

SDIT is an early-stage nonprofit. We don't have an endowment, a campus, or a large staff — we have a mission, a working plan, and a growing founding circle. A donation of any size makes the work more possible.

Everything we build is free and open. That is not incidental — it is the point. Keeping it free requires people who believe in it enough to fund it. A donation goes directly to publishing more Papers, developing the Curriculum, and supporting Students and Fellows who couldn't otherwise take part.
