IFollow the Work

Start with attention.

The lightest commitment is also the most consistent. Everything SDIT publishes is free and open — you don't need to apply, donate, or sign up to read the Curriculum or the Papers. But following along from the beginning is not nothing.

The people who are present for the founding — who read the papers as they come out, who engage with the ideas, who follow the work before it becomes well-known — those people are part of the record. The Daily brings the work to you every morning: a song, an image, an idea worth sitting with.

The Daily

A short daily email — one piece of music, one image, one idea. A habit of attention, delivered to your inbox. Free, unsponsored, no ads.

The Papers

Long-form essays on ideas worth exploring at length. Published openly, free to read, free to share. A record of what the Institute is thinking.

The open curriculum — courses, frameworks, and tools developed at SDIT — available to anyone, forever, at learn.sandiegotech.org.

IISupport the Founding

Make it possible.

SDIT is an early-stage nonprofit. We don't have an endowment, a campus, or a large staff — we have a mission, a working plan, and a growing founding circle. A donation of any size makes the work more possible.

Everything we build is free and open. That is not incidental — it is the point. Keeping it free requires people who believe in it enough to fund it. A donation goes directly to publishing more Papers, developing the Curriculum, and supporting founding Fellows who couldn't otherwise participate.

IIIThe Fellowship

The deepest level of involvement.

A Founding Fellowship is not a degree, a bootcamp, or a credential. It is an invitation to serious work — on real questions and real projects — in the company of people figuring out what it means to think and build well in the age of AI.

Fellows are not students passing through a finished curriculum. They are founders of one. What a Fellow builds becomes part of how the institution is built. There is no tuition — Fellowships stay open regardless of financial circumstance. The only requirement is genuine commitment to the work.
