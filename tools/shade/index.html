SDIT Tools · Shade · Privacy Hygiene

Know what your phone gives away.

Shade helps you do one thing well: a deep privacy cleanup once, then a quick re-check each week — with the plain-language context to understand who is actually tracking you, and how.

Free · MIT · In Testing · iOS
The ToolWhat it does

A checkup, not a scare.

Three tabs, one clear action: clean up the phone's privacy settings, understand why each step matters, and come back next week to keep it clean.

The Checkup walks you through a guided cleanup grouped by topic — tracking and ads, location, background activity, sharing, system analytics. Each step explains why it matters and gives the exact path in iOS Settings, because Apple does not let apps flip these switches for you — and an app that claims otherwise is lying to you. Shade tracks your progress, remembers when you last checked, and shows an honest live snapshot of the phone's radios and its own permissions — presented as information, not a score. The Learn tab explains how tracking actually works: ad identifiers, data brokers, in-app trackers, beacons, location trails, fingerprinting, and a risk guide for the apps everyone has.

Trust No cloud, no account, no analytics — a privacy app that itself collects nothing
Why It ExistsThe reason

Privacy is a habit, not a product.

An economy of well-funded companies profits from what your phone broadcasts. Most people never see it happen — which is exactly how it is designed.

The Institute holds that a person should command their machines, and a device that reports on you to parties you cannot name is a machine under someone else's command. Shade exists to return that authority in the most practical way we know: not paranoia, not a subscription that promises invisibility, but understanding — what is collected, by whom, why it matters — and a finite, repeatable habit for doing something about it. The tool teaches you to need it less, not more.
