IIdentity

The Founding Charter Aesthetic

SDIT's visual language is that of an institution that has existed for a long time and knows it will exist for a long time more. Every design decision should reinforce that sense: restraint over decoration, permanence over trend, character over cleverness.

The reference point is not a startup, a university marketing site, or a Silicon Valley product. It is a founding document — the kind printed on rag paper and signed with care. The look is a consequence of that intention.

VIVoice & Tone

How SDIT writes

The voice is that of an institution that has thought carefully and speaks plainly. No marketing puffery. No hedging. No casual register. Every sentence earns its place.

Register Formal but not stiff. The register of a well-written letter, not a legal brief and not a newsletter.
Sentences Short-to-medium. Declarative. Vary cadence: short statement, then expansion. Avoid passive voice.
First person "We" (institutional), never "I" in copy. Use second person "you" sparingly and only in direct address (application, donation pages).
Avoid "Innovative," "passionate," "world-class," "doing good," "change-maker," "community." These undercut seriousness.
Dates Roman numerals for years in display contexts: MMXXVI. Numeric for prose.
Em dashes Use liberally — they are the natural pause of this voice. Always surrounded by spaces.
Italics For key phrases in display text (use <em> in HTML — renders in marine blue). Avoid overuse in body copy.

Do

"Industrial education trained people to work like machines. Machines now do that work."

"We are at the beginning, deliberately."

"Every application is read personally."

Don't

"We're passionate about transforming education for the 21st century!"

"Join our innovative community of change-makers."

"Apply now and unlock your potential."

Critical framing rule

Surfing, film, photography, music, skating — these are subjects of rigorous study, not lifestyle benefits. Never present them as feature cards or "study what you love" marketing. The right framing weaves them into serious prose: "A wave is a problem in fluid dynamics and a lesson in nerve." The instant they become selling points, credibility evaporates.

VIIApplication

Applying the system

Rules for extending the brand to new surfaces — email, print, presentations, and third-party platforms.

Email White background is acceptable for email clients. Use paper (#FCFBF8) if supported. EB Garamond for subject/headline only — use system serif as fallback. IBM Plex Sans for body throughout.
Print Off-white stock preferred (100–110gsm uncoated). Navy + gold only — no screen colors. Full crest (gold version) for letterhead and cover pages. Never scale the crest below 25mm wide.
Presentations Slide background: #FCFBF8. Titles: EB Garamond 500. Body: IBM Plex Sans. Accent lines: gold hairlines. No gradients, no drop shadows, no custom shapes beyond rectangles and ruled lines.
Social Navy tile background, gold or white crest. Mono ALL CAPS label at top, Garamond quote below. No filters. No emojis in official content. Avoid social-native design patterns (rounded avatars, gradient overlays).
Third-party platforms Use the navy + gold palette wherever possible. If a platform forces its own design (e.g. PayPal button), contain it in a .give-card panel to frame it as an inset widget rather than a visual element of the page.
Photography Desaturated or naturally warm tones. No stock photography. Preference for honest documentary imagery — real people doing real work. Never posed "inspiration" photography.
What breaks the system Rounded cards, colored background panels, drop shadows, sans-serif headings, bright accent colors, gradient fills, hero imagery with text overlays, ALL-CAPS in display headings.
VIIIiOS

iOS Design Principles

The Founding Charter aesthetic translates to native iOS. The goal is an app that feels like a considered institutional tool — not a tech product — while respecting platform conventions so users are never confused by the interface itself.

Follow Apple's Human Interface Guidelines for interaction patterns, but override their visual defaults with SDIT's palette, typography, and spacing wherever the platform allows.

Color & Dark Mode

Light mode bg #FCFBF8 — use as UIColor(red:0.988 green:0.984 blue:0.973 alpha:1) or Color(hex: "FCFBF8"). Never pure white.
Dark mode bg #101C2C (ink) — the navy becomes the canvas. Secondary surfaces: #16243A. Grouped table bg: #0D1825.
Primary text Light: #101C2C. Dark: rgba(252,251,248,0.92) — warm white, not pure white. Never .label default.
Secondary text Light: #5A6472 (muted). Dark: rgba(246,245,240,0.62).
Accent / tint #234E70 (marine) — use as the app's global accentColor / tintColor. Controls links, toggles, checkboxes, selected tabs.
Gold #A8842C — labels, eyebrows, section headers, active indicators. Not the tint color (too low contrast on white).
Separator / divider Light: #D9D4C8. Dark: rgba(252,251,248,0.12). Replace all system separator colors.
Dynamic Color Define each token as a UIColor with light/dark variants. In SwiftUI use a custom Color extension. Never hardcode — always use the semantic token so dark mode is automatic.

SwiftUI — Color tokens

extension Color {
    static let sditPaper   = Color(light: Color(hex:"FCFBF8"), dark: Color(hex:"101C2C"))
    static let sditInk     = Color(light: Color(hex:"101C2C"), dark: Color(hex:"F0EFE8").opacity(0.92))
    static let sditMuted   = Color(light: Color(hex:"5A6472"), dark: Color(hex:"F0EFE8").opacity(0.50))
    static let sditMarine  = Color(light: Color(hex:"234E70"), dark: Color(hex:"4A8AB5"))
    static let sditGold    = Color(light: Color(hex:"A8842C"), dark: Color(hex:"C4A24E"))
    static let sditHairline = Color(light: Color(hex:"D9D4C8"), dark: Color(hex:"FCFBF8").opacity(0.12))
}

Typography

Display / headings EB Garamond — embed as custom font (.ttf via UIFontDescriptor or SwiftUI .custom("EBGaramond-Regular", size:)). Required for large titles and section headers.
Body IBM Plex Sans — embed or fall back to .systemFont(ofSize:weight:). Use for all prose, list items, and form labels.
Metadata / labels IBM Plex Mono — embed or fall back to .monospacedSystemFont(ofSize:weight:). Use for dates, coordinates, ALL-CAPS eyebrows, and status labels.
Dynamic Type Support Dynamic Type. Map custom fonts to system text styles using UIFontMetrics. Scale body and UI text; display/hero text may be fixed if layout requires it.
Italic emphasis EB Garamond Italic in .sditMarine for key phrases — same as web. In SwiftUI: .italic() + .foregroundColor(.sditMarine).

SwiftUI — Font tokens

extension Font {
    static func sditDisplay(_ size: CGFloat, italic: Bool = false) -> Font {
        .custom(italic ? "EBGaramond-Italic" : "EBGaramond-Medium", size: size)
    }
    static func sditBody(_ size: CGFloat = 16) -> Font {
        .custom("IBMPlexSans-Regular", size: size)
    }
    static func sditMono(_ size: CGFloat = 12) -> Font {
        .custom("IBMPlexMono-Regular", size: size)
    }
}

// Usage
Text("An institution for the")
    .font(.sditDisplay(28))
Text("Post-Industrial Renaissance")
    .font(.sditDisplay(28, italic: true))
    .foregroundColor(.sditMarine)

Navigation & Structure

Navigation bar Large title: EB Garamond, .sditInk. Bar background: .sditPaper. Bottom border: .sditHairline 1px. No blur/translucency — use opaque bars. Back button tint: .sditMarine.
Tab bar Background: .sditPaper. Selected tint: .sditMarine. Unselected: .sditMuted. Tab labels: IBM Plex Mono uppercase. Top border: .sditHairline. No system blur.
Section headers IBM Plex Mono, 11pt, #A8842C gold, ALL CAPS, tracking 0.12em — mirrors the web .article-label. Never use default grouped gray headers.
List / table rows No separator insets. Hairline separators. No system accessory chevrons unless navigating — use a custom mono arrow → in .sditMuted. Row background: .sditPaper.
Modals / sheets Detent: .large preferred. Sheet background: .sditPaper. Drag indicator: .sditHairline. Header in sheet: EB Garamond medium, not navigation bar style.
Search Search bar background: .sditPaper. Placeholder text in .sditMuted. Avoid the default gray pill shape — use an underline-only search field if custom.

Components & Controls

Buttons — primary EB Garamond italic, .sditInk, underline border — mirrors the web CTA. No filled pill buttons for primary actions. In SwiftUI: ButtonStyle with bottom border only.
Buttons — secondary IBM Plex Mono, ALL CAPS, .sditMuted with .sditHairline full border (no radius). For destructive: border in #B45309.
Cards / panels No rounded rectangle cards. Use flat panels with .sditHairline border and zero corner radius. Shadows: none. Background: .sditPaper (light) or #16243A (dark).
Status / callout Left border accent (2pt, .sditGold), no background fill. Label in IBM Plex Mono gold uppercase. Content in IBM Plex Sans — mirrors web .status component.
Toggle / switch System Toggle with .tint(.sditMarine). Label: IBM Plex Sans, .sditInk. Sublabel: IBM Plex Mono, .sditMuted, small.
Text fields Underline style only — no rounded rect border. .sditHairline 1px bottom border, thickens to .sditMarine 2px on focus. Label above field in IBM Plex Mono gold.
SF Symbols Use .monochrome rendering. Tint with .sditMarine for interactive, .sditMuted for decorative. Avoid filled variants — outline/thin weight preferred to match the engraved aesthetic.
Empty states Crest mark (sdit-shield-navy) at ~80pt, .sditMuted opacity 0.3. EB Garamond italic headline. IBM Plex Mono mono metadata line below. No illustrations.

Motion & Haptics

Transitions Prefer push/fade over custom transitions. Duration: 280–320ms with easeInOut. No spring bounces, no scale-in pop animations — they undercut the institutional tone.
Page reveals On view appear: opacity 0→1 + translateY(8pt)→0 over 400ms with .easeOut. Stagger content blocks by 80ms — matches the web .rise animation.
Haptics Use sparingly. .light impact for selections, .medium for confirmations (submit, donate). No haptics on navigation or scrolling. Never .heavy.
Reduced motion Check UIAccessibility.isReduceMotionEnabled / SwiftUI @Environment(\.accessibilityReduceMotion). Disable all translateY animations; keep opacity-only fades.

Mark construction

Concept rule One concept per mark. The symbol names the idea — not the category. A timer arc for Focus, not a clock. A shield outline for Shade, not a padlock. Ask: what is the essence of this tool?
SVG canvas viewBox="0 0 24 24" — the standard 24pt grid. Design at 24×24, export at 1024×1024 for store submission.
Stroke style stroke="currentColor", stroke-linecap="round", stroke-linejoin="round". No fills except accent dots. Stroke width: 1.75–2.2. Never go below 1.5 or above 2.5.
Optical weight The mark should read at 16px and at 512px without adjusting stroke width. If it looks heavy at small sizes, the path is too complex — simplify.
Color One color: #101C2C (ink) on paper tile. #F4F1E8 (warm paper) on navy tile for dark alternates. Never use gold, marine, or muted as the mark color — those are UI chrome.
Mark size in tile The mark occupies ~55% of the tile width. At 72px tile that is ~40px mark. At 1024px tile, set the SVG to ~560px. Center exactly — optical center and geometric center should agree.

SVG mark template

<svg viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none"
     stroke="currentColor"
     stroke-linecap="round"
     stroke-linejoin="round"
     stroke-width="1.8">

  <!-- your paths here — keep it under 4 elements -->

</svg>

App icon tile

Background #F4F1E8 — warm paper, slightly deeper than the web canvas. Keeps the mark from floating in white. For dark alternates: #101C2C.
Corner radius ~22–23% of tile width. At 1024px source: border-radius: 230px. iOS applies its own mask — don't pre-round for App Store submission. Do round for all other contexts (web, marketing, Android).
Source size 1024×1024px minimum for all store submissions. Generate all smaller sizes from this source — never scale up from smaller assets.
Display sizes 60×60pt (iOS home screen), 40×40pt (notifications), 29×29pt (settings), 1024pt (App Store). Provide all required Xcode asset catalog sizes.
No text in icon Never put the app name or any text inside the icon tile. The mark must work without labels at all sizes down to 20px.
No border/shadow The tile has no border, stroke, or drop shadow applied to the outside. The warm paper background provides sufficient separation from both light and dark home screen wallpapers.
